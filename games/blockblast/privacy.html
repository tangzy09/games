Privacy Policy

Cube Blast: Block Puzzle (com.aispeeds.cubeblast) · Last updated: 2026-07-13

Cube Blast: Block Puzzle ("the app") is a casual block puzzle game made by an independent developer. This policy explains what data the app handles.

Data we do NOT collect

The app has no account system, no registration, and no server of its own. Everything you build up while playing — level progress, stars, coins, achievements, skins, daily-puzzle streak, high score, and your in-progress game — is stored only on your device and is never uploaded by us. We cannot see it, and we do not receive it.

Advertising (Google AdMob)

The mobile app shows ads served by Google AdMob: rewarded videos you choose to watch (for a piece refresh, an undo, or coins) and occasional interstitials after completing a level. AdMob may collect and process data to serve and measure ads, including:

  • Device identifiers (such as the advertising identifier / IDFA, where you permit it)
  • Advertising interaction data and app usage data (e.g. ad views, taps)
  • Coarse location (derived from IP address)
  • Diagnostics (crash and performance data)

See Google's Privacy Policy and how Google uses data from AdMob.

Purchases

The app offers a one-time Remove Ads purchase. Payment is handled entirely by the App Store; we never receive or store your payment details. Buying it removes all interstitial ads — rewards stay available, you simply get them without watching anything.

Your choices

  • iOS App Tracking Transparency: on first launch you can allow or deny tracking. If you deny, ads are non-personalized.
  • EU/UK/Swiss users (GDPR): a consent dialog lets you accept, manage, or refuse personalized ads. You can change your choice at any time from the consent options in the app.
  • You can reset or limit your advertising identifier in your device settings at any time.
  • Deleting the app removes all locally stored progress; there is nothing on our side to delete.

Children

The app is intended for a general audience and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
